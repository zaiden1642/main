Notes


My "circle of protection" is divided into three sectors: primary (family and friends), secondary (school and neighbors), and tertiary (local community and emergency services). As you can notice, each sector has its subgroups, and priority goes clockwise, depending on the level of hazard. For instance, family (including Dad, Mom, Brother, and Biological Mom) is the first priority in the primary sector, and their respective circles have varying sizes to indicate their priority within the subgroup. The same priority rules apply to the other groups and subgroups.

I have included these people and groups in my circle because I believe they can provide me with basic needs such as food, drinking water, clothes, and shelter in case of an emergency. I also put my family and friends who are quite close to me in the circle because they can provide me with comfort in the case of a disaster.

There is actually a fourth sector to my circle of protection, and that is family and emergency services. If the hazard is too great for me and I know that it will lead to a disaster or accident, these are the contacts that will be the most useful in this type of situation.
